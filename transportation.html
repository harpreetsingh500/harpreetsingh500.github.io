Getting to Taniti

By Air

Almost all visitors arrive to Taniti by air. The island is served by a small airport that can accommodate small jets and propeller planes. Taniti is in the process of expanding the airport so larger jets will be able to land on the island within the next few years.

By Sea

Some visitors arrive on a small cruise ship that docks in Yellow Leaf Bay for one night per week. This offers a scenic and relaxed way to reach the island.

Ground Transportation

Public Buses

Public buses serve Taniti City and run from 5 a.m. to 11 p.m. every day. These buses are a convenient and affordable way to travel around the city.

Private Buses

Private buses serve the rest of the island, providing transportation to more remote areas. These buses are operated by various companies and offer scheduled services.

Taxis

Taxis are available in Taniti City and can be hailed on the street or booked via phone. They offer a comfortable and direct way to travel to your destination.

Rental Cars

Rental cars can be rented from a local rental agency near the airport. This is a great option for visitors who want the flexibility to explore the island at their own pace.

Bikes and Helmets

Bikes and helmets are available to rent from several vendors. Helmets are required by law. Taniti City is fairly flat and very walkable, making bikes a popular choice for getting around.
